Quantifiers in all-or-nothing thinking and confirmation bias

All-or-nothing thinking is a type of thinking where someone only thinks in extremes.

Thus, they might think things such as:

  • “Everyone is more sociable than me” (“for every human in the world, that human is more sociable than me”), or,
  • “No one else likes 4D Rubik’s cubes” (“for every human in the world, that human does not like the 4D Rubik’s cube”).

That is, the person says statements only using , or even worse, , where the only “solution” is them. An example of the latter is “I am the only one who actually cares about [list some cause here that has an entire organization dedicated to helping it]”, or in terms of , “ human in the world that cares about [list some cause here that has an entire organization dedicated to helping it]. That human is me”.

Yet, another type of thought that is also annoying, especially when combined with all-or-nothing thinking, is confirmation bias. For example, I might see a single instance of someone who dropped out of university and then became a millionaire, and use that to conclude everyone who drops out of university becomes a millionaire.
