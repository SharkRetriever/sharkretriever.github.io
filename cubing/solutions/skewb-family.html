Skewb Family

I don’t like speedsolving skewbs, but the skewb’s a pretty interesting puzzle geometrically.

Skewb

I didn’t learn this myself, so not posting tutorial here. However, the general outline for solving (not the quickest way though) I use is:

  1. First layer corners and center
  2. Opposite corners with repeated sledgehammer
  3. Opposite center with repeated sledgehammer
  4. Side centers with sledgehammer-based algs

Skewb Diamond

This puzzle was a lot easier than I expected.

Solving Overview

  1. Solve corners
  2. Solve top and bottom centers (First two centers)
  3. Last six centers

Note that we’ll hold the puzzle the same way as the face-turning octahedron and that the first step will be the same.

Step 1: Solve Corners

See face-turning octahedron. Scroll down to its step 1.

Step 2: First Two Centers

We use sledgehammer (R’ L’ R L) to put the first and second centers into place. Note that doing sledgehammer three times preserves corners, so we’ll call sledgehammer done three times Algorithm A. Note that Alg A swaps the U and F centers, and thus make sure that the side you want to solve is the U-side, and that the side with the center piece you need is the F-side before doing the alg. After solving the U side, flip the puzzle 180 so that the U and D faces have switched positions, and repeat, making sure that the pieces are in the right place first.

Step 3: Last Six Centers

It turns out the last six centers are just solving last three centers twice; there’s clearly two cycles of order 3. Thus, if your puzzle is solved, or if you seem to only have three centers left to solve, that’s completely normal. Place the three centers you need to solve in the F, BR, and D faces and do sune (R U R’ U R U R’ U) to swap F->BR->D or antisune (R’ U’ R U’ R’ U’ R U’) to swap F->D->BR

Rotate the puzzle and repeat with the last three centers if originally, all of the last six centers were unsolved.

Master Skewb

This seems like a fun puzzle. Heard its solving method is a lot different than that of the skewb, though.
