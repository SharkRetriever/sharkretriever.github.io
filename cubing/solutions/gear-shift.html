Gear Shift

General Puzzle Notes

I’ve solved this puzzle by accident as many times as I have solved it on purpose. However, knowing a solution is a lot better than blindly hoping that the gears will shift themselves into the right place after a certain number of twists.

It turns out that each big gear can be turned in increments of two teeth, making it order 4.
Each small gear can be turned in increments of one tooth, making it order 5.

Solution found: Feb 26, 2016

General Solving Overview

We will use a slightly different notation here. X, where X is a layer, will now represent splitting the puzzle so that the X-layer can be turned on its own. Since X is capitalized, it represents turning a big gear in that layer by 360 degrees clockwise. When x is lowercase, it represents turning a small gear in that layer by 360 degrees clockwise.

We will also use Xm to denote turning a large gear in layer X by any amount of teeth. We’ll give a condition for when to stop turning, no worries. Also note that “corner” and “gear” will be used synonymously, as will “large” and “big”.

  1. Solve first 6 pieces
  2. Solve last small gear
  3. Solve last big gear

Method

Step 1: Solve First Six (or More) Pieces

This can be done quite intuitively. First, pick one big corner and twist it so that at least it (the corner) is solved. Take a look at the neighbouring pieces’ stickers to determine whether it has been solved. Place this corner in UFL. Then, the puzzle can be split by all three axes, one at a time, so that Rm, Dm, and Bm turns can be done until the ULB, DLF, and URF corners, respectively, are solved.

Then, do a z’ turn and do Um turns until the ULB and UFL corners are solved. Repeat both the rotation and the Um one more time.

At worst, the UBR and URF corners both won’t be solved. At best, both of those corners will be solved.

Step 2: Solve Last Small Gear

If the UFR small gear isn’t solved, place it in the URF position, and count by how many teeth it would have to rotate by counterclockwise in order to be solved. Let n equal this number. Then do R*n U*n B’*n.

Step 3: Solve Last Big Gear

If there is one big gear that isn’t solved, place it in the UBR position, and count by how many teeth it would have to rotate by counterclockwise in order to be solved. Divide this by two, and let n equal this number. Then do r*n u*n f’*n. Remember that lowercase layers indicate turning the small gear instead of the big gear.
