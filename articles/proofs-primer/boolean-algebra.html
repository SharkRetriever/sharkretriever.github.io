Boolean Algebra

Whereas (high-school) algebra involves manipulating expressions of real numbers or polynomials, Boolean algebra, introduced by Boole, manipulates logical expressions.

In math, the theorems that must be proven are all statements. Informally, a (logical) statement is a piece of information that is either true or false. For example, “1 + 1 = 2” is a statement that is true, “1 + 1 = 5” is a statement that is false, and “Are we there yet?” is a question, but not a logical statement.
