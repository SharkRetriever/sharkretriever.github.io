Tamagotchi Logic

Whereas the Numbers game serves to remind one not to indulge too much in an activity solely for the rankings (unless they can actually put those rankings to good use), Tamagotchi logic is a method that I have found for improving my life satisfaction, beyond just going for rankings.

Tamagotchi logic involves a few steps to implement:

  1. List out topics that represent the parts of life that matter the most (to you)
  2. Visualize the bars for each topic, and think about how often they need to be refilled
  3. When upset, visualize the bars, and see which one is low

The first step is important, because any bars that show up beyonds food, water, and shelter, will likely reveal character or interests. The bars should form a summary of your needs and wants, and are best kept private.

The second step is also worth mentioning, because bars empty at different rates. A water bar may empty every hour, whereas a food bar only three times a day, for example. Knowing the rates at which the bars empty allow one to re-prioritize their day, improving their overall satisfaction.

The third step is the most important though; once the bars have been constructed, the first step, when upset, now simply involves thinking up each bar, and seeing which one is empty. For example, I often realized that I became irritable when my “(fresh) air” bar was empty, meaning that I was staying in one place, and/or staying inside, for too long.

Though these steps are simple, remembering to bring them up is likely the hardest part. The bars also cannot always help one who is upset, but can at least help them identify their problem a majority of the time, assuming they have configured their bars correctly.
