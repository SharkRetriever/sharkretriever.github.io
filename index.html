About Me

I am a third-year University of Waterloo computer science student who takes problems such as “I can’t find an empty room to study in”, and converts them into software such as the app UWatALoner. Depending on the desired output format, I might produce scripts instead, such as this scraper that predicts competitor rankings for a given Canadian Rubik’s cube competition, based on available past results.

When it comes to hobbies, mine are undeniably tied to mathematics. In particular, I enjoy figuring out solutions to four-dimensional Rubik’s cubes, and I am eager to produce some microtonal trance in 31-ET as soon as I set up (or program) some software for assistance.

Moving up several thousand feet, my life is minimalistic. For example, I have given away or thrown out over half of my Rubik’s cube collection. I also try to purchase long-term material less often, even when such material is on sale.

My goals for now are to push my insights on math and music theory onto this site, toss in some of my current and previous software development projects, and add a dash of philosophical thoughts. In the meantime, check out the highlights below!

Highlights on this site:

  • Courses I hope to take at the University of Waterloo – along with the courses I actually took.
  • N-dimensional A permutations – a set of algorithms that solves any N-dimensional analogue of 3x3x3 Rubik’s cube.
  • A Primer on Proofs – learn to articulate your reasoning through the use of implications and quantifiers. For both aspiring mathematicians and non-mathematicians alike!
  • The Numbers Game – a game I have played for much too long.
