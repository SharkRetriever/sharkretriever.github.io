Site and Paper Recommendations

These are the ones I’ve got so far:

  • 4D Euclidean Space is full of original pictures and content, dissecting each polychoron (4D polytope) layer by layer so you can see how they’re all constructed. The site also provides a great intro to 4-dimensional geometry in general and helps with visualizing duoprisms!
  • Visualizing Hyperbolic Hypercombs is full of renderings of various hyperbolic tilings. Also deals with non-compact hypercombs, such as the {6,3,3} hexagonal tiling honeycomb.
  • Dot Net Perls uses comprehensible examples for various concepts in C#, including more abstract ones such as Actions and delegates. Oh wait, we can just use lamdbas now!
  • Arch Chinese is amazing if you know how to speak Mandarin, but completely suck at reading and writing it.

Awesome Quick Reads

  • Tabular Method for Integration by Parts – they don’t teach this method in MATH 138 at the University of Waterloo, but it’s a nice way to evaluate certain integrals.
  • Acoustic Phonetics – a ten-minute read on how human speech can be formatted as spectrograms. Especially interesting is the “Formants” section which relates patterns in the spectrograms to the IPA vowel trapezium.
